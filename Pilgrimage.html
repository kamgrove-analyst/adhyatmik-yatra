The Char Dham Yatra, also known as the "Chota Char Dham" to distinguish it from the major Char Dham pilgrimage encompassing Badrinath, Dwarka, Puri, and Rameswaram, is a revered Hindu pilgrimage circuit nestled in the Garhwal Himalayas of Uttarakhand, India.
It comprises four holy shrines:
1.Yamunotri: The source of the Yamuna River and the seat of Goddess Yamuna.
2.Gangotri: The origin of the Ganges River and the abode of Goddess Ganga.  
3.Kedarnath: Dedicated to Lord Shiva and one of the twelve Jyotirlingas.  
4.Badrinath: The sacred abode of Lord Vishnu.
History and Significance:
The formation of the Char Dham Yatra in Uttarakhand is attributed to the 8th-century reformer and philosopher Adi Shankaracharya. This pilgrimage is considered highly sacred by Hindus, and it is believed that undertaking this journey helps cleanse the soul of sins and attain moksha (liberation) from the cycle of birth and death. Each of the four sites is associated with unique religious significance and legends, drawing millions of devotees annually.
The Traditional Route:
Traditionally, the Yatra is undertaken in a clockwise direction, starting from Yamunotri, then proceeding to Gangotri, followed by Kedarnath, and finally concluding at Badrinath. However, pilgrims can customize their itinerary based on their preferences and available time. En route, pilgrims often visit other holy places like Haridwar and Rishikesh.
